  1. Pick two categorical variables and make a visualization to visualize the relationship between the two variables. Along with your code and output, provide an interpretation of the visualization.
ggplot(starwars) + 
  geom_bar(aes(y = sex)) +
  facet_wrap(. ~ species)

It’s a bit difficult to view this chart, but one can see that the vast majority of species only have males. Of species with females, most other than humans are fairly equal. We can see here the humans are the largest species, followed by droids, followed by Gungans.

  1. Pick two numerical variables and two categorical variables and make a visualization that incorporates all of them and provide an interpretation with your answer.

subset(starwars, name != "Jabba Desilijic Tiure") %>%
  drop_na(sex) %>%
  ggplot() +
    geom_point(aes(x = height, y = mass, color = birth_year)) +
    facet_grid(. ~ sex)
Warning: Removed 25 rows containing missing values (`geom_point()`).

We can see that interestingly droids come in three sets. Most females are around the same weight/mass, with one outlier. Males have a much larger spectrum.
